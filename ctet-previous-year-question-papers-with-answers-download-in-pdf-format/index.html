Syllabus and Exam Pattern for CTET

Applicants should familiarise themselves with the CTET Syllabus and Exam Pattern before sitting the exam, as this will help them prepare better for the exam. When preparing for the CTET, it is a good idea to go over the curriculum thoroughly. There are five subjects that must be covered for CTET Paper-I: English Language, Hindi Language, Child Development and Pedagogy, Environmental Studies, and Mathematics.

Social Studies, Science, English Language (including Hindi), Child Development & Pedagogy, Environmental Studies (including Mathematics), and Mathematics are among the subjects covered in the CTET Paper-II. Knowing the test pattern assists us to get a better understanding of the type and difficulty of questions that will be asked on the exam.

What is the level of difficulty for CTET Papers I and II?

The difficulty level of CTET Paper I and II is moderate, making it easy to moderate. The questions in CTET Paper I will be based on the themes covered in the NCERT syllabus for classes I – V. Nevertheless, the difficulty level of the questions may be comparable to that of secondary school.

The key focus of CTET Paper II-Mathematics and Science and Social Studies/Social Science, respectively, will concentrate on ideas, problem-solving abilities, instructional understanding, and applications of the disciplines. For grades VI-Volume 1, the questions will be organised into different divisions of the subject's syllabus, as established by the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT).

In CTET, what kind of negative marking is there?

CTET does not use a system of negative marking. It is recommended that applicants attempt all questions in the exam for the best chance of getting above-average marks.

Is the CTET Certificate valid for a certain amount of time?

From the date of issue, the CTET certificate is valid for seven years.

What are the types and levels of difficulty of the CTET questions?

The nature and standard of questions in CTET are linked to the topic studied; for example, Child Development and Pedagogy will focus on the educational psychology of teaching and learning that pertains to children aged six to eleven years.

In the future, the necessity of understanding the characteristics and requirements of various learners, as well as contact with learners and the characteristics and qualities of a competent learning facilitator, will be stressed further. The topics of Mathematics and Environmental Studies will focus on the ideas, problem-solving abilities, pedagogical knowledge, and applications prescribed by the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) for grades I through V.
